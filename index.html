Sourcing Strategies

Your Competition Does

Elite sourcers don't wait for applications. They build pipelines, map talent, and engage passive candidates months before a role opens.

Multi-Channel Sourcing
Strategy 01

Multi-Channel Sourcing That Consistently Delivers

The best technical recruiters never rely on one platform. A robust sourcing strategy combines multiple channels — each with its own approach, tone, and outreach cadence — to maximise reach and response rates across the market.

  • LinkedIn Recruiter advanced filters and saved talent pools
  • GitHub and GitLab open-source contributor mapping
  • Stack Overflow Talent and developer community forums
  • Boolean X-Ray search across Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo
  • Employee referral amplification programmes
  • Conference speaker lists and technical blog authors
Talent Mapping
Strategy 02

Talent Mapping & Pipeline Building

Stop sourcing reactively. Build talent maps for critical roles 3–6 months before you need them, so you're never starting from zero when hiring urgency strikes. Proactive pipelines consistently outperform reactive searches.

  • Competitor talent mapping and org chart analysis
  • Skills-based pipeline segmentation in your ATS
  • Warm nurture sequences for passive candidates
  • CRM tagging and talent intelligence platforms
  • Market benchmarking and salary intelligence
  • Re-engagement strategies for silver medal candidates
Delivering Candidates
Strategy 03

Delivering Great Candidates Every Time

Sourcing is only half the job. Delivering high-quality shortlists that hiring managers genuinely trust requires process discipline, quality screening, and clear candidate positioning at every stage of the hiring funnel.

  • Structured intake meetings to align on must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
  • Competency-based phone screens that save valuable interview time
  • Candidate scorecards aligned directly to role requirements
  • Transparent briefing documents for every submission
  • Active candidate management throughout the process
  • Post-offer support to close candidates and handle counteroffers
DEI Strategies

Build Diverse,
Inclusive Engineering Teams

Diversity isn't a checkbox — it's a competitive advantage. Here's how the world's leading tech companies embed inclusion into every stage of technical hiring.

01

Inclusive Job Descriptions

Eliminate gendered language, unnecessary requirements, and jargon that deters underrepresented candidates. Use tools like Textio to continuously improve JDs before publishing.

02

Diverse Sourcing Channels

Partner with Black Tech Pipeline, Lesbians Who Tech, and Latinos in Tech. Source from HBCUs, coding bootcamps, and Return-to-Work programmes to reach untapped talent pools.

03

Structured Interviews

Standardise questions, use scorecards, and train interviewers to recognise unconscious bias. Consistent processes produce fairer outcomes and consistently better-quality hires.

04

Blind Resume Screening

Remove names, addresses, and graduation years from early-stage reviews. Focus evaluation on skills, experience, and measurable impact — not demographic proxies or pedigree signals.

05

Pay Equity Frameworks

Implement transparent salary bands, conduct annual pay equity audits, and train recruiters to make offers based on the role level and market data — never on salary history.

06

DEI Metrics & Accountability

Track diversity at every funnel stage. Measure panel diversity, offer rates by demographic, and attrition patterns to identify exactly where your pipeline is losing underrepresented talent.

LinkedIn Sourcing Mastery

Most recruiters only scratch the surface of what LinkedIn can do. These are the strategies used by elite technical sourcers to consistently outperform the market.

01

Boolean Operators in LinkedIn Search

Combine AND, OR, NOT with parentheses to build precision queries. Example: (Python OR Django) AND (AWS OR GCP) NOT "engineering manager"

02

Skills Filters & Endorsement Signals

High endorsement counts from respected peers signal genuine community recognition — especially valuable for niche technical skills that are hard to verify through CVs alone.

03

InMail Personalisation at Scale

Reference a specific post, project, or article the candidate has published. Personalised InMails achieve 3–5× higher response rates than generic outreach templates.

04

Projects & Saved Search Alerts

Organise talent pools by role and seniority. Set weekly alerts to automatically surface newly active candidates matching your target profile before competitors reach them.

05

Open to Work & Passive Candidates

Filter for candidates signalling openness — but never ignore passive candidates. The highest-quality technical hires are typically not actively job searching at all.
