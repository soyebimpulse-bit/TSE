Sourcing Strategies

Find Talent Before Your Competition Does

Elite sourcers don't wait for applications. They build pipelines, map talent, and engage passive candidates at scale before a role even opens.

Multi-Channel Sourcing That Delivers

The best technical recruiters don't rely on one platform. A robust sourcing strategy combines multiple channels — each with its own approach, tone, and outreach cadence — to maximise reach and response rates.

  • LinkedIn Recruiter advanced filters and saved talent pools
  • GitHub and GitLab open-source contributor mapping
  • Stack Overflow Talent and developer community forums
  • Boolean X-Ray search across Google, Bing, and DuckDuckGo
  • Employee referral amplification and incentive programmes
  • Conference speaker lists and technical blog authors

Talent Mapping & Pipeline Building

Stop sourcing reactively. The most effective technical recruiters build talent maps for critical roles 3–6 months in advance, so they're never starting a search from zero when urgency strikes.

  • Competitor talent mapping and org chart analysis
  • Skills-based pipeline segmentation in your ATS
  • Warm nurture sequences for passive candidates
  • CRM tagging workflows and talent intelligence tools
  • Market benchmarking and real-time salary intelligence
  • Re-engagement strategies for silver medal candidates

Delivering Great Candidates

Sourcing is only half the job. Delivering high-quality shortlists that hiring managers trust requires process discipline, quality screening, and clear candidate positioning at every stage.

  • Structured intake meetings to align on must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
  • Competency-based phone screens that save interview time
  • Candidate scorecards aligned to role requirements
  • Transparent briefing documents for every submission
  • Active candidate management throughout the interview process
  • Post-offer support to close candidates and handle counteroffers
DEI Strategies

Build Diverse, Inclusive Engineering Teams

Diversity isn't a checkbox — it's a competitive advantage. Here's how the world's leading tech companies embed inclusion into every stage of technical hiring.

Inclusive Job Descriptions

Eliminate gendered language, unnecessary requirements, and jargon that deters underrepresented candidates. Use tools like Textio to score and continuously improve your JDs before publishing.

Diverse Sourcing Channels

Partner with organisations like Black Tech Pipeline, Lesbians Who Tech, and Latinos in Tech. Source from HBCUs, coding bootcamps, and Return-to-Work programmes to reach untapped talent pools.

Structured Interviews

Standardise interview questions, use scorecards, and train interviewers to recognise unconscious bias. Consistent processes produce fairer outcomes and consistently better-quality hires.

Blind Resume Screening

Remove names, addresses, and graduation years from early-stage CV reviews. Focus evaluation entirely on skills, experience, and measurable impact — not demographic proxies or pedigree signals.

Pay Equity Frameworks

Implement transparent salary bands, conduct annual pay equity audits, and train recruiters to make offers based on the role level and market rate — never on a candidate's salary history.

DEI Metrics & Accountability

Track diversity at every funnel stage — not just at hire. Measure panel diversity, offer rates by demographic, and attrition patterns to identify exactly where your pipeline is losing underrepresented talent.

LinkedIn Sourcing Mastery

LinkedIn is the world's largest professional network — but most recruiters only scratch the surface. These are the strategies used by elite technical sourcers to consistently outperform.

01

Boolean Operators in LinkedIn Search

Combine AND, OR, NOT with parentheses to build precision queries that find exactly the profile you need. Example: (Python OR Django) AND (AWS OR GCP) NOT "engineering manager"

02

Skills Filters & Endorsement Signals

Use the Skills filter to narrow by verified expertise. High endorsement counts from respected peers signal genuine community recognition — especially valuable for niche technical skills.

03

InMail Personalisation at Scale

Reference a specific post, GitHub project, or article the candidate has published. Personalised InMails consistently achieve 3–5x higher response rates than generic outreach templates.

04

Projects & Saved Searches

Create talent pools organised by role, seniority, and skills. Set up saved searches with weekly alerts to automatically capture newly active candidates matching your target profiles.

05

Open to Work & Career Interests

Filter for candidates who have signalled openness to new opportunities — but never ignore passive candidates. The highest-quality technical hires are typically not actively job searching.
